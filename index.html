Contemplative psychology

Contents:
Contemplative psychology "is a psychology that forms an intrinsic part of the contemplative traditions of most world religions. The term 'contemplative psychology' therefore does not refer to academic psychological theory about contemplation, religion or religious behavior. It refers to the psychological insights and methods that are - often implicitly - present in the vision and practice of religions and that clarify and guide ones contemplative or religious development" (p. 82).[1] "Contemplative psychology addresses the question of how we could intelligently approach and understand human life-experience" (p. 83).[1]
